3 · Monitoring playbook — track the thesis, not the ticker

A concentrated sleeve is won or lost on behavior, not stock-picking. The discipline below exists to stop two specific mistakes: (1) reacting to price noise on a 4.5-year position, and (2) letting a sleeve drawdown panic you into touching the 90% indexed core. The rule is simple — you sell when the reason you bought stops being true, not when the price moves.

The principle
For every holding, hold a one-liner: "I own ___ because ___; the one thing that would break this is ___." Review that sentence against each earnings report. If the breaker hasn't happened, the price move is noise — do nothing.
The cadence
Two 30-min reviews a month — the 1st & the 15th. Not daily price. On a 4-year horizon, daily price is ~pure noise. Each holding's WHEN row tells you which review it lands in. Ignore the tape in between.
The one macro screen
The capex tripwire governs ~85% of the book at once: any top-4 hyperscaler (MSFT/GOOG/META/AMZN) guiding capex flat-or-down, AI-revenue run-rates stalling, or DRAM/NAND spot rolling over. One screen, checked quarterly, covers the correlated bet.
Explicitly ignore (noise, not signal): analyst price-target changes · single-day moves · CNBC/X hot-takes · one product launch · "stock up/down X% today" · a single broker downgrade. None of these change a 2030 thesis. The four wrong reasons people actually sell — price dropped, I'm up and scared, scary headline, boredom — are all banned. 1 · Thematic thesis mapping — Aschenbrenner's "Situational Awareness"

The framework decomposes AI progress into three multiplicative OOM drivers: raw compute (~0.5 OOM/yr), algorithmic efficiency (~0.5 OOM/yr), and "unhobbling" (agents, tools, reasoning — step-changes worth 5–30×). Stacked, they imply ~5 more OOMs of effective compute by end-2027 — a GPT-2→GPT-4-sized leap every couple of years, on straight-line extrapolation. The investable consequence is not "which model wins" but who supplies the physical scaffolding of a 100,000× scale-up.

The roadmap forces the constraint ranking. Training clusters march from ~1 GW today toward ~100 GW and $1 trillion+ by 2030 — north of 20% of total US electricity generation, which has grown barely 5% in a decade:

YearOOMs vs GPT-4H100-equiv.Cluster costPower
2024+1~100k$ billions~100 MW
2026+2~1 million$10s B~1 GW
2028+3~10 million$100s B~10 GW
2030+4~100 million$1 trillion+~100 GW

The shift this encodes: GPUs are not the late-decade gating factor — power is, then datacenter construction/permitting and advanced packaging (CoWoS/HBM), with raw wafers a manageable third. As clusters run flat-out 24/7, the binding question becomes "where do I find 10 gigawatts?" — and the bandwidth to wire 100k+ GPUs into one coherent fabric. That is why this portfolio rotates capital out of crowded compute and into power, thermal, and optical interconnect.
